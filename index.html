What is the purpose of this page?

It is indeed interesting to know if an ALS patient condition is stable or deteriorating.
Until 2018 there was little known biomarkers for ALS.
Thanks to analysis of the ALS database PRO-ACT, scientists learned that some common biomarkers may indicate how a patient situation is evolving.

Here we use a simplified interpretation of their data. There are two types of ALS, slow and fast evolving.
So any biomarker level lay in one of three areas, worse than the fast evolving condition, better than the slow progression, or in between.
It is also interesting to know how the biomarker is evolving: Does it stay flat or it is worsening? So we ask for two values for each biomarker.

We do not store any data, so we are compliant with EU's GDPR. Our indications are extremely simplified with respect to the original publication.

The publication used for our calculations is : Predicting functional decline and survival in amyotrophic lateral sclerosis
Mei-Lyn Ong , Pei Fang Tan , Joanna D. Holbrook

We are not medical doctors, nor biologists, so if you have any question, ask to your MD

Alkaline phosphatase

The normal value for alkaline phosphatase is 53 to 128 U/L for a 20- to 50-year-old man and 42 to 98 U/L for a 20- to 50-year-old woman. Normal values are slightly different if you are older than 60, or pregnant.

Creatine Kinase

The muscle cells in your body need CK to function. If this test shows that your CK levels are high, you may have muscle damage. Normal values at rest are usually between 60 and 174 IU/L.
